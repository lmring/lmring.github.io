Weapons

There is a lot of combat in Dungeons & Dragons, and in order to fight, you’re going to need weapons. Weapons have two basic classifications: whether they are melee or ranged weapons, and whether they are simple or martial weapons. For example, a weapon could be a simple melee weapon or a martial ranged weapon. Once you understand weapons, Lily will explain how to attack with them!

Weapon Range: Melee or Smash-- I mean, Ranged

There are two weapon ranges, melee and ranged.

Melee Weapons

  • Attack a target that is within five feet of you
  • Use the user's Strength modifier unless a finesse weapon
  • Favored by sturdy characters who have enough bulk to be in the frontlines

Ranged Weapons

  • Attack a target that is further than five feet of you
  • Use the user's Dexterity modifier unless a thrown weapon
  • Favored by squishier characters who want to stay out of harm's way
  • Sometimes require ammunition

Weapon Style: Simple? Martial? Sounds Complex!

Beyond a weapon’s range, there’s the weapon’s style. Simple weapons do not require any kind of training to use. Martial weapons are more powerful, and require specialized training.

Simple Weapons

Most classes are proficient in simple weapons. They often used by spellcasters (wizards, warlocks, druids, etc.) who have some other way of inflicting damage. Some spellcaster specializations allow proficiency in martial weapons, so be sure to check!

Martial Weapons

Martial weapons are mostly used by warriors (fighters, rangers, rogues, etc). They are more powerful because they tend to be a primary way of inflicting damage.

Weapon Properties: Make the Most of Your Weapons!

Some weapons have special properties related to their use. Listed below are some of the most common properties you will encounter--see external resources for the full list!

Ammunition: You need ammunition for this weapon in order to use it, like arrows for a shortbow.

Finesse: You can choose to use your dexterity modifier instead of your strength modifier when using this weapon

Heavy: Small creatures, like halflings and gnomes, have disadvantage when using heavy weapons. They’re just too heavy!

Thrown: You can choose to use your strength modifier instead of your dexterity modifier when using this weapon. Favored by strength based characters who need a way of attacking ranged!

Two-Handed: You need two hands to use this weapon. This often means the weapon cannot be used at the same time as a shield.

Versatile: You can use this weapon with one hand or with two hands. It will do more damage if you use two hands!

Improvised Weapons: Anything. Really, anything! You could even use a dead goblin as an improvised weapon. These weapons always do 1d4 damage, unless the DM decides otherwise.

How to Attack

To attack, you will need to roll a D20, then add the appropriate ability modifier and your proficiency bonus. If the result exceeds your opponent’s Armor Class (AC), then you will roll for damage, which varies depending on the specific weapon.
