Backgrounds

Backgrounds explain who your character was before they became an adventurer. Which background you choose defines some proficiencies, languages, equipment, and features.

Having a background is essential for roleplaying. As the official handbook suggests, think about what changed. Why is your character an adventurer now, instead of staying with their background role?

Check Out Backgrounds!

Acolyte

Proficiencies: Insight, Religion

Languages: Two of your choice

Equipment: A holy symbol, a prayer book or prayer wheel, 5 sticks of incense, vestments, a set of common clothes, and a pouch with 15 gp

Feature: Shelter of the Faithful - You and your companions receive healing, care, and shelter at temples of your faith. If you have ties to a specific temple, you can call upon it for more serious assistance when nearby.

Charlatan

Proficiencies: Deception, Sleight of Hand, disguise kit, forgery kit

Equipment: A set of fine clothes, a disguise kit, tools of the con of your choice (ten stoppered bottles filled with colored liquid, a set of weighted dice, a deck of marked cards, or a signet ring of an imaginary duke), and a pouch with 15 gp

Feature: False Identity - You have a second identity with documentation, established acquaintances, and documentation that allow you to assume that persona. You can also forge documents once you have seen a sample of what you are trying to copy.

Criminal

Proficiencies: Deception, Stealth, one type of gaming set, thieves’ tools

Equipment: A crowbar, a set of dark common clothes including a hood, and a pouch with 15 gp

Feature: riminal Contact - You have a reliable contact that connects you to a network of other criminals. You can get messages to and from this contact even over great distances.

Entertainer

Proficiencies: Acrobatics, Performance, disguise kit, one type of musical instrument

Equipment: A musical instrument of your choice, the favor of an admirer (love letter, lock of hair, or trinket), a costume, and a pouch with 15 gp

Feature: By Popular Demand - You can always find a place to perform, and will receive free lodging and food there as long as you perform each night.

Folk Hero

Proficiencies: Animal Handling, Survival, one type of artisan’s tools, land vehicles

Equipment: A set of artisan’s tools of your choice, a shovel, an iron pot, a set of common clothes, and a pouch with 10 gp

Feature: Rustic Hospitality - You can find a place to hide, rest, or recuperate among other commoners, who will shield you from the law or people searching for you.

Guild Artisan

Proficiencies: Insight, Persuasion, one type of artisan’s tools

Languages: One of your choice

Equipment: A set of artisan’s tools of your choice, a letter of introduction from your guild, a set of traveler’s clothes, and a pouch with 15 gp

Feature: Guild Membership - Your guild will provide you with lodging, food, a meeting place for other members of your profession, and political power. You must pay dues of 5 gp per month to your guild.

Hermit

Proficiencies: Medicine, Religion, herbalism kit

Languages: One of your choice

Equipment: A scroll case stuffed full of notes from your studies or prayers, a winter blanket, a set of common clothes, an herbalism kit, and 5 gp

Feature: Discovery - The quiet seclusion of your hermitage gave you access to a unique and powerful discovery. Work with your DM to determine the details of your discovery and its impact on the campaign.

Noble

Proficiencies: History, Persuasion, one type of gaming set

Languages: One of your choice

Equipment: A set of fine clothes, a signet ring, a scroll of pedigree, and a purse with 25 gp

Feature: Position of Privilege - Thanks to your noble birth, people are inclined to think the best of you. People assume you have the right to be wherever you are and the common folk make every effort to accommodate you.

Outlander

Proficiencies: Athletics, Survival, one type of musical instrument

Languages: One of your choice

Equipment: A staff, a hunting trap, a trophy from an animal you killed, a set of traveler’s clothes, and a pouch containing 10 gp

Feature: Wanderer - You can always recall the general layout of terrain, settlements, and other features around you. You can also find food and fresh water for yourself and up to five other people each day, provided that the land is suitable for this.

Sage

Proficiencies: Arcana, History

Languages: Two of your choice

Equipment: A bottle of black ink, a quill, a small knife, a letter from a dead colleague posing a question you have not been able to answer, a set of common clothes, and a pouch with 10 gp

Feature: When you attempt to learn or recall a piece of lore, if you do not know it, you often know where and from whom you can obtain it. This is at the DM’s discretion, and they can rule that a piece of knowledge is unfindable.

Sailor

Proficiencies: Athletics, Perception, navigator’s tools, water vehicles

Equipment: A belaying pin, 50 ft of silk rope, a lucky charm, a set of common clothes, and a pouch with 10 gp

Feature: Ship’s Passage - When you need to, you can secure free passage on a sailing ship for yourself and your companions, in return for assisting the crew during the voyage.

Soldier

Proficiencies: Athletics, Intimidation, one type of gaming set, land vehicles

Equipment: An insignia of rank, a trophy taken from a fallen enemy (dagger, broken blade, or piece of a banner), a set of bone dice or deck of cards, a set of common clothes, and a pouch with 10 gp

Feature: Military Rank - You can use your rank to exert influence over other soldiers, requisition simple equipment or horses, and gain access to friendly military encampments.

Urchin

Proficiencies: Sleight of Hand, Stealth, disguise kit, thieves’ tools

Equipment: A small knife, a map of the city you grew up in, a pet mouse, a token to remember your parents by, a set of common clothes, and a pouch with 10 gp

Feature: City Secrets - When you are not in combat, you and your companions can travel between any two locations in the city twice as fast as your speed would normally allow.
